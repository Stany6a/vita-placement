How does senior placement assistance work?

A senior placement advisor helps families compare assisted living communities, board and care homes, and memory care options based on care needs, budget, preferred location, and personal preferences. Instead of contacting dozens of communities on your own, you receive guidance, recommendations, and support through the decision process.

Why work with a senior placement advisor?

Finding the right senior living option can feel overwhelming. A placement advisor helps narrow the search, explain different levels of care, arrange tours, and identify communities that may be a good fit for your loved one.

What is assisted living and who is it for?

Assisted living is designed for older adults who want to remain as independent as possible but need support with daily activities such as bathing, dressing, medication management, meals, or mobility. Communities typically provide private or shared apartments, meals, social activities, housekeeping, and staff assistance throughout the day.

What is memory care?

Memory care is a specialized type of senior living for people with Alzheimer’s disease, dementia, or other forms of memory loss. Memory care communities offer secure environments, structured daily routines, specially trained staff, and personalized support designed around each resident’s cognitive and physical needs.

What is a board and care home?

A board and care home is a smaller residential care setting, usually located in a traditional house and serving a limited number of residents. These homes often provide a quieter environment, home-style meals, personal assistance, and a higher level of individual attention than larger senior living communities.

What does senior living include?

Senior living is a broad term that includes different housing and care options for older adults, such as independent living, assisted living, memory care, and board and care homes. The right option depends on a person’s health, level of independence, care needs, budget, preferred location, and lifestyle.

Is senior placement assistance really free for families?

Yes. Vita Placement Assistance is 100% free to families. We are compensated by the senior living community after a successful placement, so families do not pay a fee for our guidance, recommendations, or help arranging tours.

How much do assisted living, memory care, and board and care homes cost?

Senior living costs vary based on the type of care, location, room style, level of assistance, and services included. Assisted living, memory care, and board and care homes may each use different pricing structures. Vita Placement Assistance helps families understand these costs, compare options, and identify communities that fit their care needs and budget.

Do I need to visit every senior living community myself?

Can Vita help arrange senior living tours?

Yes. We help families coordinate tours of selected senior living communities and prepare for the questions that are important to ask. This makes it easier to compare care, services, pricing, atmosphere, and availability before making a decision.
